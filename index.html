Impact of SGLT2 Inhibitors on Cardiac Remodeling in Heart Failure with Preserved Ejection Fraction

Journal of Cardiac Failure · Original Article · Draft

Introduction

Heart failure with preserved ejection fraction (HFpEF) represents approximately half of all heart failure cases and is associated with significant morbidity and mortality [Chen et al., 2023]. Unlike heart failure with reduced ejection fraction (HFrEF), effective pharmacological treatments for HFpEF have remained elusive until recently.

The emergence of sodium-glucose cotransporter 2 (SGLT2) inhibitors has transformed the therapeutic landscape. The landmark EMPEROR-Preserved trial demonstrated that empagliflozin reduced the combined risk of cardiovascular death or hospitalization for heart failure by 21% [Anker et al., 2021], while the DELIVER trial showed similar benefits with dapagliflozin [Solomon et al., 2022].

However, the mechanisms underlying the cardioprotective effects of SGLT2 inhibitors in HFpEF remain incompletely understood. Proposed mechanisms include improvements in myocardial energetics, reduction in interstitial fibrosis, attenuation of oxidative stress, and favorable effects on cardiac loading conditions [Verma et al., 2023].

Methods

We conducted a prospective, single-center cohort study of patients diagnosed with HFpEF (ejection fraction ≥ 50%) who were initiated on SGLT2 inhibitor therapy between January 2022 and December 2024. Cardiac magnetic resonance imaging was performed at baseline and at 12 months to assess changes in myocardial structure and function.

Primary inclusion criteria were: (1) confirmed HFpEF diagnosis with LVEF ≥ 50% on echocardiography within 6 months, (2) NYHA functional class II-III, (3) elevated NT-proBNP (>125 pg/mL), and (4) initiation of either empagliflozin 10mg or dapagliflozin 10mg daily.

Statistical Analysis

Continuous variables were compared using paired t-tests or Wilcoxon signed-rank tests as appropriate. Changes in CMR parameters were analyzed using mixed-effects models with time as a fixed effect and patient as a random effect. A two-sided p-value <0.05 was considered statistically significant.

Cardiac MRI Protocol for SGLT2 Inhibitor Assessment in HFpEF

S. Singh1, A. Kumar2
March 2026
Abstract
This supplementary document details the cardiac magnetic resonance imaging protocol used to assess myocardial structural and functional changes in patients with heart failure with preserved ejection fraction (HFpEF) treated with SGLT2 inhibitors. We describe acquisition parameters, post-processing methods, and quality assurance procedures.

1   Introduction

Cardiac magnetic resonance (CMR) imaging is the gold standard for non-invasive assessment of myocardial structure and function [Kramer et al., 2020]. Recent advances in quantitative mapping techniques have enabled detailed tissue characterization, including measurement of native T1, T2, and extracellular volume (ECV) fraction [Messroghli et al., 2017].

2   Methods

2.1   Study Design

This was a prospective, single-center cohort study approved by the institutional review board (IRB #2022-0847). Written informed consent was obtained from all participants.

2.2   MRI Protocol

All examinations were performed on a 3T scanner (MAGNETOM Prisma, Siemens Healthineers) using a 32-channel cardiac coil. The protocol included:

ECV = (1 − Hct) · ΔR1myo / ΔR1blood

where Hct is the hematocrit and ΔR1 represents the change in longitudinal relaxation rate.
